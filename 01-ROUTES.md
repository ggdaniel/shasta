# Mount Shasta — Route Guide

> See also: [Ski Descents](10-SKI-DESCENTS.md) for a detailed descent-specific comparison of all lines, and [Weather](02-WEATHER.md) for the freeze-thaw cycle that determines corn timing.

## Route 1: Avalanche Gulch ⭐ RECOMMENDED for May

**The classic. Most popular route on the mountain. Best conditions mid-May.**

### Profile
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Trailhead | Bunny Flat (6,950 ft) — see [Permits & Logistics](04-PERMITS-LOGISTICS.md#trailhead-access--bunny-flat) |
| Distance | ~11.5 mi round trip |
| Elevation gain | ~7,200 ft |
| Duration | 8–14 hours summit day (from Helen Lake) |
| Difficulty | Strenuous — Class 2 snow climb |
| Technical | Crampons + ice axe required. No ropes needed. See [Gear List](03-GEAR-LIST.md). |
| Crowds | Moderate to high in May — the trade-off for the best route |

### Route Description

**Bunny Flat (6,950 ft) → Horse Camp (7,880 ft)**
- ~1.8 mi through forest on Sierra Club trail
- Well-marked, moderate grade
- Horse Camp has a stone cabin (Sierra Club), seasonal caretaker, spring water
- Good rest/refuel point
- See [Camping options](04-PERMITS-LOGISTICS.md#camping) for Horse Camp details

**Horse Camp → Helen Lake (10,443 ft)**
- ~2.5 mi up Avalanche Gulch snowfield
- This is the long grind — 2,500 ft of sustained snow climbing
- Moderate angle, but relentless
- Helen Lake is THE camp spot — flat snow benches, wind-protected options
- Most teams camp here, wake at midnight–2 AM for summit push
- See [Snow Camping Deep Dive](09-DEEP-DIVES.md#2-snow-camping-at-helen-lake--the-full-breakdown) for platform building, anchoring, and camp setup

**Helen Lake → The Heart / Red Banks (12,000 ft)**
- Steeper terrain begins
- "The Heart" — large heart-shaped rock feature, good landmark
- Red Banks — the crux: a band of volcanic rock cliffs
- In mid-May, typically climb through a chute in the Red Banks (35–40° snow)
- **Rockfall zone** — helmets mandatory, climb early when frozen
- See [Safety: Rockfall](06-SAFETY.md#3-rockfall--icefall) for timing and mitigation

**Red Banks → Misery Hill (13,000 ft)**
- Above Red Banks, terrain mellows briefly
- Possible sulfur smell from fumaroles near the summit plateau
- Cross a flat area, then up "Misery Hill" — a deceptive slog at altitude
- See [Safety: Altitude Illness](06-SAFETY.md#5-altitude-illness) for what to expect at this elevation

**Misery Hill → Summit (14,179 ft)**
- Final push across the summit plateau
- Summit pinnacle is a small rocky area
- **Navigation critical in whiteout** — easy to wander off wrong side
- [GPS waypoints for the full route](08-RESOURCES.md#key-waypoints-to-program-into-gps)

### Ski Descent Notes
- **Avalanche Gulch is the #1 ski descent on Shasta.** 5,000+ vertical feet from Red Banks to Bunny Flat. Full breakdown in [Ski Descents: Avalanche Gulch](10-SKI-DESCENTS.md#descent-1-avalanche-gulch--the-classic--recommended).
- Mid-May corn snow: overnight freeze firms the surface, morning sun softens it into perfect corn by 8–9 AM. This is the window. See [Weather: Freeze-Thaw Cycle](02-WEATHER.md#the-freezethaw-cycle--critical-for-summit-day-timing).
- Skin up from Bunny Flat through Horse Camp and up the gulch to Helen Lake. Continue skinning or transition to bootpack as angle steepens approaching Red Banks. See [Gear List: Transition Plan](03-GEAR-LIST.md#-ski-transition-plan--where-you-ski-vs-bootpack).
- **Stash skis at base of Red Banks** (~12,000 ft) and bootpack with crampons + ice axe to summit.
- Ski down from Red Banks. Wide open gulch, few obstacles, sustained pitch.
- Below Helen Lake the angle mellows — cruise through to Horse Camp and thread the forest trail back to Bunny Flat.
- **Timing is everything:** Ski the descent at 8–9 AM for ideal corn. Too early = ice. Too late = mush + wet slides.

### Why Choose This for May 17
- Best snow coverage in mid-May — smooth, consolidated snow surface
- Prime corn snow skiing conditions — the freeze-thaw cycle is dialed in May
- Rockfall risk lower than summer (snow covers loose rock)
- Long daylight (sunrise ~5:50 AM, sunset ~8:20 PM) — see [Weather](02-WEATHER.md)
- Most [trip reports and beta](05-TRIP-REPORTS.md) available for this route
- Easiest access (Bunny Flat road typically open by mid-May) — see [Permits & Logistics](04-PERMITS-LOGISTICS.md)
- Continuous snow coverage to trailhead = ski all the way to the car

## Route 2: Clear Creek

**The south-side alternative. Less crowded. More hiking, less climbing feel.**

### Profile
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Trailhead | Clear Creek trailhead (6,600 ft) — south side. [Road access uncertain in May](04-PERMITS-LOGISTICS.md). |
| Distance | ~12 mi round trip |
| Elevation gain | ~7,600 ft |
| Difficulty | Strenuous — Class 2 |
| Technical | Crampons + ice axe in May; more scree/hiking later season. [Gear List](03-GEAR-LIST.md). |

### Character
- Less crowded than Avalanche Gulch
- In May: still significant snow coverage, similar feel to Avy Gulch
- By July: mostly scree and volcanic ash — tedious
- Less rockfall exposure than Avalanche Gulch (no Red Banks crux)
- Access road may be snowed in earlier in May — check conditions

### Why / Why Not
- ✅ Fewer people, quieter experience
- ✅ No Red Banks rockfall gauntlet
- ❌ Trailhead access less certain in mid-May (road may be snowy)
- ❌ Less dramatic scenery
- ❌ Fewer [trip reports](05-TRIP-REPORTS.md) for conditions beta

## Route 3: Hotlum-Bolam Ridge

**The mountaineer's route. Remote, quieter, real alpine feel. See [Ski Descents: Hotlum-Bolam](10-SKI-DESCENTS.md#descent-4-hotlum-bolam--north-side) for the descent.**

### Profile
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Trailhead | North Gate trailhead (~7,000 ft) — north side |
| Distance | ~14 mi round trip |
| Elevation gain | ~7,200 ft |
| Difficulty | Strenuous — Class 3 |
| Technical | Crampons, ice axe, rope, glacier gear recommended. [Gear List](03-GEAR-LIST.md). |

### Character
- North side of the mountain — longer approach
- Glacier travel possible (Hotlum Glacier and Bolam Glacier)
- Crevasse hazard exists — rope team recommended
- More complex route-finding
- Spectacular views, far fewer climbers

### Why / Why Not
- ✅ True alpine mountaineering experience
- ✅ Very few people — possible to have the route to yourself
- ✅ More technical challenge
- ❌ Requires glacier travel skills and equipment
- ❌ Longer approach, more complex logistics
- ❌ Less beta available, harder to bail
- ❌ Not recommended for first Shasta attempt

## Route 4: West Face / Cascade Gulch

**Steeper, more direct lines for experienced mountaineers. See [Ski Descents: Cascade Gulch](10-SKI-DESCENTS.md#descent-3-cascade-gulch--west-face).**

- 40–50° sustained snow/ice
- Rarely climbed in May (avalanche risk)
- Not recommended for a first Shasta trip
- Mentioned for completeness

## Recommendation for Your Team

**Avalanche Gulch.** No question for a mid-May ski descent with a 3-person team. It's the classic for a reason:
- Best snow conditions of the year — prime corn skiing
- 5,000+ vertical feet of skiing from Red Banks to the car — [full descent breakdown](10-SKI-DESCENTS.md#descent-1-avalanche-gulch--the-classic--recommended)
- Most well-documented route with the most ski descent beta
- Technical enough to be real mountaineering but not requiring glacier skills
- Camp at Helen Lake, summit push starting 1–2 AM, ski down at 8–9 AM in perfect corn
- See the full [Itinerary](07-ITINERARY.md) for the day-by-day plan
