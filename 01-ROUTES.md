# Mount Shasta — Route Guide

## Route 1: Avalanche Gulch ⭐ RECOMMENDED for May

**The classic. Most popular route on the mountain. Best conditions mid-May.**

### Profile
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Trailhead | Bunny Flat (6,950 ft) |
| Distance | ~11.5 mi round trip |
| Elevation gain | ~7,200 ft |
| Duration | 8–14 hours summit day (from Helen Lake) |
| Difficulty | Strenuous — Class 2 snow climb |
| Technical | Crampons + ice axe required. No ropes needed. |
| Crowds | Moderate to high in May — the trade-off for the best route |

### Route Description

**Bunny Flat (6,950 ft) → Horse Camp (7,880 ft)**
- ~1.8 mi through forest on Sierra Club trail
- Well-marked, moderate grade
- Horse Camp has a stone cabin (Sierra Club), seasonal caretaker, spring water
- Good rest/refuel point

**Horse Camp → Helen Lake (10,443 ft)**
- ~2.5 mi up Avalanche Gulch snowfield
- This is the long grind — 2,500 ft of sustained snow climbing
- Moderate angle, but relentless
- Helen Lake is THE camp spot — flat snow benches, wind-protected options
- Most teams camp here, wake at midnight–2 AM for summit push

**Helen Lake → The Heart / Red Banks (12,000 ft)**
- Steeper terrain begins
- "The Heart" — large heart-shaped rock feature, good landmark
- Red Banks — the crux: a band of volcanic rock cliffs
- In mid-May, typically climb through a chute in the Red Banks (35–40° snow)
- **Rockfall zone** — helmets mandatory, climb early when frozen

**Red Banks → Misery Hill (13,000 ft)**
- Above Red Banks, terrain mellows briefly
- Possible sulfur smell from fumaroles near the summit plateau
- Cross a flat area, then up "Misery Hill" — a deceptive slog at altitude

**Misery Hill → Summit (14,179 ft)**
- Final push across the summit plateau
- Summit pinnacle is a small rocky area
- **Navigation critical in whiteout** — easy to wander off wrong side

### Why Choose This for May 17
- Best snow coverage in mid-May — smooth, consolidated snow surface
- Rockfall risk lower than summer (snow covers loose rock)
- Long daylight (sunrise ~5:50 AM, sunset ~8:20 PM)
- Most trip reports and beta available for this route
- Easiest access (Bunny Flat road typically open by mid-May)

## Route 2: Clear Creek

**The south-side alternative. Less crowded. More hiking, less climbing feel.**

### Profile
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Trailhead | Clear Creek trailhead (6,600 ft) — south side |
| Distance | ~12 mi round trip |
| Elevation gain | ~7,600 ft |
| Difficulty | Strenuous — Class 2 |
| Technical | Crampons + ice axe in May; more scree/hiking later season |

### Character
- Less crowded than Avalanche Gulch
- In May: still significant snow coverage, similar feel to Avy Gulch
- By July: mostly scree and volcanic ash — tedious
- Less rockfall exposure than Avalanche Gulch (no Red Banks crux)
- Access road may be snowed in earlier in May — check conditions

### Why / Why Not
- ✅ Fewer people, quieter experience
- ✅ No Red Banks rockfall gauntlet
- ❌ Trailhead access less certain in mid-May (road may be snowy)
- ❌ Less dramatic scenery
- ❌ Fewer trip reports for conditions beta

## Route 3: Hotlum-Bolam Ridge

**The mountaineer's route. Remote, quieter, real alpine feel.**

### Profile
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Trailhead | North Gate trailhead (~7,000 ft) — north side |
| Distance | ~14 mi round trip |
| Elevation gain | ~7,200 ft |
| Difficulty | Strenuous — Class 3 |
| Technical | Crampons, ice axe, rope, glacier gear recommended |

### Character
- North side of the mountain — longer approach
- Glacier travel possible (Hotlum Glacier and Bolam Glacier)
- Crevasse hazard exists — rope team recommended
- More complex route-finding
- Spectacular views, far fewer climbers

### Why / Why Not
- ✅ True alpine mountaineering experience
- ✅ Very few people — possible to have the route to yourself
- ✅ More technical challenge
- ❌ Requires glacier travel skills and equipment
- ❌ Longer approach, more complex logistics
- ❌ Less beta available, harder to bail
- ❌ Not recommended for first Shasta attempt

---

## Route 4: West Face / Cascade Gulch

**Steeper, more direct lines for experienced mountaineers.**

- 40–50° sustained snow/ice
- Rarely climbed in May (avalanche risk)
- Not recommended for a first Shasta trip
- Mentioned for completeness

---

## Recommendation for Your Team

**Avalanche Gulch.** No question for a mid-May date with a 3-person team. It's the classic for a reason:
- Best snow conditions of the year
- Most well-documented route
- Technical enough to be real mountaineering but not requiring glacier skills
- Camp at Helen Lake, summit push starting 1–2 AM
