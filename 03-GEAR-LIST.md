# Mount Shasta — Complete Gear List (3-Person Team, May, Ski Descent)

> See also: [Boot Strategy Deep Dive](09-DEEP-DIVES.md#1-ski-boot-strategy-wear-the-whole-time-vs-carry--switch) for whether to wear AT boots the whole time, [Snow Camping Deep Dive](09-DEEP-DIVES.md#2-snow-camping-at-helen-lake--the-full-breakdown) for shelter setup, and [Waste Management Deep Dive](09-DEEP-DIVES.md#3-human-waste-management-the-practical-guide) for WAG bag details.

## ⛏️ Technical Gear — NON-NEGOTIABLE

These items have no substitutes. If you don't have them, don't go.

| Item | Notes | Own/Rent |
|------|-------|----------|
| **AT / randonee ski boots** | Crampon-compatible, walk mode for skinning, ski mode for descent. Must fit your skis' bindings. | Own |
| **AT / randonee skis** | Sized for touring — lighter than resort skis. Wide enough for variable spring snow (~90–105mm waist). | Own |
| **AT bindings** | Tech (pin) or frame bindings with walk mode. Must release reliably. | Own |
| **Climbing skins** | Fitted to your skis. Trim before the trip. Bring skin wax (prevents glopping in warm snow). | Own |
| **Ski crampons / couteaux** | Attach to bindings for icy skin tracks. Critical for the steep upper gulch on the ascent. | Own |
| **Boot crampons (12-point)** | Steel, fitted to your ski boots. For bootpacking above where you stash skis (Red Banks +). Microspikes are NOT a substitute. | Own/rent |
| **Ice axe** | Standard 60–75cm. For bootpacking the upper mountain + self-arrest. Practice before the trip. | Own/rent |
| **Climbing helmet** | Hard shell. Mandatory above Helen Lake. Rockfall from Red Banks is real. Doubles as ski helmet on descent. | Own |
| **Ski poles** | Adjustable touring poles. Extended for skinning, shortened for skiing. | Own |

## 🎿 Ski-Specific Gear

| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|
| **Ski straps** | For carrying skis on pack during bootpack section |
| **Skin wax / glop stopper** | Mid-May snow can be sticky. Prevents skins from clogging with wet snow. |
| **Ski goggle (low light + bright)** | Pre-dawn ascent = dark lens or clear. Descent in sun on snow = dark/mirror lens. Bring both or a photochromic. |
| **Ski-specific backpack (40–50L)** | Must have A-frame or diagonal ski carry, helmet holder, avy gear pocket. Osprey Soelden, Black Diamond Dawn Patrol, or similar. |
| **Boot bag / crampon bag** | Keep crampons from shredding your pack. |
| **Ski boot crampons adapter** | Verify your 12-point crampons fit your AT boots. Test this at home. Some AT boots need specific crampon models. |

## ⚠️ Ski Transition Plan — Where You Ski vs. Bootpack

| Section | Mode | Notes |
|---------|------|-------|
| Bunny Flat → Horse Camp | Skin | Forest trail. Moderate grade. |
| Horse Camp → Helen Lake | Skin | Avalanche Gulch snowfield. Sustained moderate angle. Skins work great. |
| Helen Lake → Red Banks (~12,000 ft) | Skin or bootpack | Steeper. Ski crampons helpful. May need to bootpack the last 500 ft depending on conditions. |
| Red Banks chute | **Bootpack** | Too steep to skin (~35–40°). Stash skis at base of Red Banks or carry on pack. |
| Red Banks → Misery Hill → Summit | **Bootpack** | Crampons + ice axe. Skis on pack or stashed at Red Banks. |
| Summit → Red Banks | **Bootpack down** or ski if conditions allow | Retrieve skis if stashed. |
| Red Banks → Helen Lake | **SKI** | The reward. 2,000+ ft of sustained skiing. Corn snow if timed right. |
| Helen Lake → Horse Camp | **SKI** | Wide open gulch. Mellow angle. Fast descent. |
| Horse Camp → Bunny Flat | **SKI** | Through forest on trail. Snow coverage usually holds to trailhead in mid-May. |

**The descent from Red Banks to Bunny Flat is ~5,000 vertical feet of skiing.** What takes 3–4 hours to hike up takes 30–45 minutes to ski down.

## 🧥 Clothing — Layering System

Ascent starts at 1 AM in 10°F, ski descent at 8–9 AM in 30–40°F. You need both warm and breathable.

### Base Layer
- [ ] Moisture-wicking top (merino wool or synthetic)
- [ ] Moisture-wicking bottoms (merino or synthetic)
- [ ] Liner socks (synthetic or thin wool)
- [ ] Ski-weight socks (cushioned toe/heel — Darn Tough or Smartwool ski)

### Mid Layer
- [ ] Fleece or light down jacket (insulating layer)
- [ ] Softshell jacket (wind-resistant, breathable — great for skinning)

### Outer Layer
- [ ] Hardshell jacket — waterproof, windproof, full hood, pit zips, powder skirt or drawcord hem
- [ ] Ski / hardshell pants — waterproof, side zips, reinforced cuffs (crampon + ski edge resistant)
- [ ] Gaiters — if pants don't seal well over boots

### Extremities
- [ ] Warm hat / balaclava (fits under helmet)
- [ ] Lightweight liner gloves (for skinning)
- [ ] Insulated ski gloves (waterproof — for descent and summit push)
- [ ] Spare pair of gloves (wet gloves = cold hands = done)
- [ ] Glacier glasses (Category 4 UV) — for skinning
- [ ] Ski goggles (for descent + wind/whiteout) — photochromic or dual lens
- [ ] Buff / neck gaiter
- [ ] Sun hat (optional — for skinning in warm temps)

### Summit Push Extra
- [ ] Heavyweight down or synthetic puffy (for rest stops, summit, emergencies, and if waiting at ski stash)

## 🎒 Backpack & Shelter

| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|
| **Ski touring pack (40–50L)** | Ski carry, helmet holder, avy pocket. Use for approach + camp carry. |
| **Summit pack (25–35L)** | Lighter pack for summit push from Helen Lake (if stashing skis at Red Banks, pack skis on this) |
| **4-season tent** | Must handle wind. 3-person for 3 people. Stake it well. |
| **Sleeping bag** | Rated to 10–15°F. Down or synthetic. |
| **Sleeping pad** | R-value 5+ for snow camping. Insulated inflatable or closed-cell foam. |
| **Snow stakes / deadman anchors** | Regular tent stakes won't hold in snow. Bring snow stakes or bury stuff sacks. |

## 🍳 Food & Water

| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|
| **Stove + fuel** | White gas or canister. Cold temps affect canister performance — keep warm in sleeping bag. |
| **Pot + mug** | Melt snow for water (camp is on snow) |
| **Water bottles (2x 1L)** | Wide-mouth Nalgene — won't freeze shut. Keep inside jacket on summit day. |
| **Water treatment** | For spring water at Horse Camp. Snow melt above is clean. |
| **Food — approach/camp** | Pasta, ramen, oatmeal, snacks. You're burning 4,000–6,000 cal/day. |
| **Food — summit day** | Easy to eat in cold: energy bars, gels, gummy bears, trail mix, jerky. Nothing that freezes solid. |
| **Electrolytes** | Altitude + exertion = dehydration. Liquid IV, Nuun, etc. |

**Calorie plan:** Eat 200–300 cal per hour on summit day. Eat before you're hungry. Drink before you're thirsty.

| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|

| **Compass** | Backup navigation. Summit plateau whiteouts are real. |

## ❄️ Avalanche Safety — REQUIRED for Ski Descent

Skiing the descent makes avalanche gear **mandatory**, not optional.

| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|
| **Avalanche beacon** | 3-antenna digital. Fresh batteries. Practice search before trip. Each person carries one. |
| **Avalanche probe** | 240cm minimum. Know how to assemble quickly. |
| **Avalanche shovel** | Metal blade. Know companion rescue protocol. |
| **Inclinometer** | On phone or compass. Measure slope angles. |

**All three team members must carry beacon, probe, and shovel. Practice a companion rescue scenario before the trip.** See [Safety](06-SAFETY.md) for full hazard breakdown.

Avalanche risk on descent:
- Skiing at 8–9 AM = low risk (snow still consolidated from overnight freeze)
- Skiing at 11 AM+ = rising risk (wet loose slides, solar warming)
- This is another reason to summit early and descend early
