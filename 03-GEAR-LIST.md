# Mount Shasta — Complete Gear List (3-Person Team, May)

## ⛏️ Technical Gear — NON-NEGOTIABLE

These items have no substitutes. If you don't have them, don't go.

| Item | Notes | Own/Rent |
|------|-------|----------|
| **Mountaineering boots** | Crampon-compatible, insulated, waterproof. NOT hiking boots. | Rent if needed |
| **Crampons (12-point)** | Steel, strap-on or step-in. Must fit your boots. Test before trip. Microspikes are NOT a substitute. | Rent if needed |
| **Ice axe** | Standard 60–75cm mountaineering axe. Practice self-arrest before the climb. | Rent if needed |
| **Climbing helmet** | Hard shell. Mandatory above Helen Lake. Rockfall from Red Banks is real. | Own/rent |
| **Trekking poles** | For approach and descent. Collapsible. Snow baskets. | Own |

## 🧥 Clothing — Layering System

Summit day: start at 1 AM in 10°F, finish at noon in 40°F. You need everything.

### Base Layer
- [ ] Moisture-wicking top (merino wool or synthetic)
- [ ] Moisture-wicking bottoms (merino or synthetic)
- [ ] Liner socks (synthetic or thin wool)
- [ ] Mountaineering socks (thick wool — Darn Tough or Smartwool)

### Mid Layer
- [ ] Fleece or light down jacket (insulating layer)
- [ ] Softshell jacket (wind-resistant, breathable — optional but nice)

### Outer Layer
- [ ] Hardshell jacket — waterproof, windproof, full hood, pit zips
- [ ] Hardshell pants — waterproof, side zips to fit over boots/crampons
- [ ] Gaiters — keep snow out of boots (critical for deep snow)

### Extremities
- [ ] Warm hat / balaclava (fits under helmet)
- [ ] Lightweight liner gloves
- [ ] Insulated mountaineering gloves (waterproof)
- [ ] Spare pair of gloves (wet gloves = cold hands = done)
- [ ] Glacier glasses (Category 4 UV) — sunglasses are NOT enough
- [ ] Ski goggles (for wind/whiteout conditions)
- [ ] Buff / neck gaiter
- [ ] Sun hat (for approach)

### Summit Day Extra
- [ ] Heavyweight down or synthetic puffy (for rest stops, summit, emergencies)

## 🎒 Backpack & Shelter

| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|
| **Pack (50–65L)** | For overnight camp at Helen Lake |
| **Summit pack (30–40L)** | Lighter pack for summit day if leaving camp set up |
| **4-season tent** | Must handle wind. 3-person for 3 people. Stake it well. |
| **Sleeping bag** | Rated to 10–15°F. Down or synthetic. |
| **Sleeping pad** | R-value 5+ for snow camping. Insulated inflatable or closed-cell foam. |
| **Snow stakes / deadman anchors** | Regular tent stakes won't hold in snow. Bring snow stakes or bury stuff sacks. |

## 🍳 Food & Water

| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|
| **Stove + fuel** | White gas or canister. Cold temps affect canister performance — keep warm in sleeping bag. |
| **Pot + mug** | Melt snow for water (camp is on snow) |
| **Water bottles (2x 1L)** | Wide-mouth Nalgene — won't freeze shut. Keep inside jacket on summit day. |
| **Water treatment** | For spring water at Horse Camp. Snow melt above is clean. |
| **Food — approach/camp** | Pasta, ramen, oatmeal, snacks. You're burning 4,000–6,000 cal/day. |
| **Food — summit day** | Easy to eat in cold: energy bars, gels, gummy bears, trail mix, jerky. Nothing that freezes solid. |
| **Electrolytes** | Altitude + exertion = dehydration. Liquid IV, Nuun, etc. |

**Calorie plan:** Eat 200–300 cal per hour on summit day. Eat before you're hungry. Drink before you're thirsty.

## 🧭 Navigation & Safety

| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|
| **GPS device or phone with offline maps** | Gaia GPS, AllTrails, CalTopo. Download maps before. |
| **Compass** | Backup navigation. Summit plateau whiteouts are real. |
| **Headlamp + spare batteries** | You're starting at 1–2 AM. Bring two or bring extra batteries. Cold kills batteries. |
| **Whistle** | Emergency signaling |
| **Sunscreen SPF 50+** | Reapply every 2 hours. UV on snow is brutal. |
| **Lip balm with SPF** | Your lips will destroy if unprotected |
| **First aid kit** | Ibuprofen (altitude headache), blister care, tape, gauze, antiseptic |
| **Emergency bivy / space blanket** | Lightweight, could save your life if stuck |

## 📡 Communication

| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|
| **Cell phone** | Service at trailhead (spotty). No service above Helen Lake. |
| **Satellite communicator** | Garmin inReach Mini or similar. SOS capability. Highly recommended for a 3-person team without guides. |
| **Two-way radios** | Optional but useful if team separates on summit day. |

## 🏔️ Optional but Recommended

- [ ] Avalanche beacon + probe + shovel — recommended in May per Shasta Avalanche Center
- [ ] Camera (GoPro, phone)
- [ ] Hand/toe warmers — for summit push
- [ ] Pee bottle — saves leaving tent at 3 AM on a snow field
- [ ] Sit pad — insulates your ass from snow during breaks
- [ ] Duct tape (wrapped around pole or bottle) — universal fix
- [ ] Knife / multitool
- [ ] Stuff sacks / dry bags — organize and waterproof gear

## 🚗 Car / Basecamp Gear

- [ ] Cooler with post-climb food and cold drinks
- [ ] Camp chairs
- [ ] Change of clothes for drive home
- [ ] Sandals / camp shoes
- [ ] Cash for summit pass ($25/person)
