## GPX Files & Route Tracks

### Avalanche Gulch (Primary Route)

**Gaia GPS — Mount Shasta via Avalanche Gulch**
- URL: https://www.gaiagps.com/hike/244931/mount-shasta-via-avalanche-gulch-climbing-route-and-hotlum-bolum-ridge/
- Download: GPX and KML available (click "GPX/KML" button on page)
- Stats: 14.5 mi, 7,024 ft elevation gain
- Waypoints include: Bunny Flat, Horse Camp, Helen Lake, The Heart, Red Banks, Misery Hill, Summit

**Gaia GPS — Avalanche Gulch Specific Track**
- URL: https://www.gaiagps.com/public/yJ6j9LvkOInFSStwheC4LbcD/?embed=True
- Embed/download available

**CalTopo — Create Custom Route**
- URL: https://caltopo.com/
- Use satellite + slope angle layers to plan your exact line
- Can export GPX directly
- Recommended: create your own route and share with team

**AllTrails — Avalanche Gulch**
- Search "Mount Shasta Avalanche Gulch" on AllTrails
- Multiple recorded tracks with elevation profiles
- AllTrails+ required for offline GPX download

## Training Before the Trip

### Physical Preparation (Start Now — 4 Weeks Out)
See [Safety: Altitude Illness](06-SAFETY.md#5-altitude-illness) for what your body will face above 10,000 ft.
- **Cardio:** Stair climber, incline treadmill, uphill hiking with a weighted pack (30–40 lbs)
- **Legs:** Squats, lunges, step-ups, calf raises
- **Core:** Planks, Russian twists (core stabilizes on steep snow)
- **Endurance:** 4–6 hour hikes on weekends with full pack
- **Target:** Be comfortable hiking 8+ hours with a 35 lb pack and 4,000+ ft elevation gain

### Skills to Practice
- [ ] Self-arrest with ice axe (all positions — head up, head down, face up, face down)
- [ ] Walking in crampons on varied terrain
- [ ] Rest-step technique (for efficient high-altitude walking)
- [ ] Snow camping setup (if new to it)
- [ ] GPS navigation (program waypoints, follow track)
