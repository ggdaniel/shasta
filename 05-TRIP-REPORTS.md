## 2025 Season Summary (from Shasta Avalanche Center, May 7, 2025)

**Key takeaways from the most recent climbing advisory:**

> "The mountain experienced an above average winter and coverage is consistent down to 6,500 feet. Prime-time climbing season is now through June. Don't delay until July or later; while some routes are accessible later in the season, spring offers the ideal conditions. Long days, pleasant weather, low avalanche danger and rockfall potential and smooth, supportable surfaces all contribute to good climbing conditions."

**Hazards noted (in order of frequency):**
1. **Icy surfaces / slip and fall** — Most common rescue cause every year. Upper mountain is steep, firm snow. Self-arrest is a must.
2. **Poor weather / whiteout** — Climbers get lost on summit plateau every year. Cloud caps form on clear days.
3. **Rockfall / icefall** — Red Banks area. Early spring: icefall from rime ice. Late spring/summer: rock loosened by snowmelt.
4. **Avalanches** — Wind slabs and wet-loose instabilities. Carry beacon, shovel, probe.

**Direct quote from climbing rangers:**
> "Don't take Mount Shasta lightly. Despite being only 15 minutes off the Interstate, it's a real mountain with real hazards. It can kill you."

### What to Look For in Trip Reports (1–2 Weeks Before)

- **Snow conditions at Helen Lake:** Firm? Icy? Deep? Is there a good platform for tents?
- **Red Banks status:** Is the chute open? How steep? Ice or snow?
- **Rockfall activity:** Time of day rocks started falling. How bad?
- **Wind conditions on summit day:** What time did wind pick up?
- **Turnaround time:** When did teams decide to turn back and why?
- **Bootpack/trail:** Is there a clear bootpack? Or breaking trail?
- **Glissade conditions:** Can you glissade down from Helen Lake? (Huge time saver on descent)

## Common Patterns from Historical May Trip Reports

### Things That Go Right
- Leave Helen Lake at 1 AM, summit by 7 AM, back at camp by 10 AM. Textbook.
- Firm snow all the way up. Crampons bite perfectly.
- Clear skies, light wind. Can see Mt. Lassen, Trinity Alps, even the coast.
- Glissade from Helen Lake back to Horse Camp in 30 minutes (instead of 2 hours hiking).

### Things That Go Wrong
- **Started too late.** Left at 4 AM, hit soft snow at Red Banks, falling rocks, turned back at 12,500 ft.
- **Whiteout on summit plateau.** Clear at Helen Lake, cloud cap formed by 8 AM. Navigated by GPS. Scary.
- **Altitude sickness.** Felt fine at Helen Lake. Couldn't eat at 12,000 ft. Turned back at 13,000 ft.
- **Wind.** Forecast said 20 mph. Actual was 50 mph at Red Banks. Couldn't stand. Turned back.
- **Equipment failure.** Crampon came loose on the descent in steep terrain. Near-disaster.
- **Underestimated descent.** Tired after summit, sloppy foot placement, slipped on firm snow above Red Banks. Long slide.

### Key Pattern
**Almost every "went wrong" report involves starting too late or ignoring weather signs.** The mountain rewards early starts and punishes complacency. See the [Itinerary](07-ITINERARY.md) for the summit day timeline designed around these lessons.
