## On the Mountain — Elevation-Based Conditions

### Bunny Flat Trailhead (6,950 ft)
- Temperature: 45–55°F daytime, 25–35°F overnight
- Snow depth: typically 3–6 ft in mid-May
- Road: Everitt Memorial Hwy usually OPEN by mid-May
- Conditions: soft afternoon snow, firm morning crust

### Helen Lake Camp (10,443 ft)
- Temperature: 30–40°F daytime, 10–20°F overnight
- Wind: 10–25 mph typical, gusts to 40+ mph possible
- Snow depth: deep, consolidated snowpack
- Conditions: freeze-thaw cycle drives everything — firm/icy before dawn, softening by 10 AM
- UV: extreme at this elevation on snow — sunburn in minutes without protection

### Summit (14,179 ft)
- Temperature: 10–25°F typical, can drop below 0°F with wind
- Wind: 20–40 mph average, gusts 60+ mph in storms
- Wind chill: can reach -20°F or colder
- Oxygen: ~60% of sea level
- Conditions: firm snow to bulletproof ice pre-dawn; softening after sunrise

## The Freeze-Thaw Cycle — Critical for Summit Day Timing

This is the single most important weather concept for your climb:

1. **Overnight (midnight–5 AM):** Snow surface freezes hard. Crampons bite well. Rockfall minimal (rocks frozen in place). This is why you start early.
2. **Morning (5–9 AM):** Snow begins softening in direct sun. Still firm on shaded aspects. Best summit window.
3. **Late morning (9 AM–noon):** Snow becomes soft and sloppy. Rockfall increases dramatically (sun loosens rocks). Post-holing begins. Avalanche risk rises.
4. **Afternoon (noon+):** Worst conditions. Wet snow, active rockfall, possible afternoon thunderstorms. You should be well off the upper mountain by noon.

**Bottom line: Leave Helen Lake at 1–2 AM. Summit by 8–9 AM. Be below the Red Banks by 10 AM. Back to Helen Lake by noon.**

## Historical Mid-May Conditions (Typical Year)

- **Snowpack:** Above average to average. 2025 was above average. Coverage consistent down to 6,500 ft.
- **Storm frequency:** 1–2 storm systems per month in May. Storms can drop 6–12 inches of new snow above 8,000 ft.
- **Window between storms:** Typically 3–7 day windows of stable weather.
- **Cloud caps / lenticulars:** Common. The mountain creates its own weather. A clear sky in town does NOT mean clear sky on the summit.
- **Lightning:** Rare but possible in May. More common June–August.

## Go / No-Go Criteria

**GO conditions:**
- Clear to partly cloudy forecast for summit day
- Winds below 30 mph at summit elevation
- No active storm system within 24 hours
- Stable freeze-thaw cycle (cold overnight, warming morning)

**NO-GO conditions:**
- Active precipitation on summit day
- Summit winds forecast above 40 mph sustained
- Whiteout / zero visibility conditions
- Active avalanche warning
- Rapidly warming temperatures (rain at Helen Lake elevation = very bad)
- Lenticular cap clouds forming on summit — storm indicator

## Week of May 17 — What to Expect

Mid-May is generally considered **prime climbing season** for Shasta. You're in the sweet spot:
- Snow is consolidated from winter but not yet melting out
- Days are long (14.5 hours of daylight)
- Storm frequency is decreasing from spring peak
- Freeze-thaw cycle is reliable
- Rockfall risk is lower than summer (snow covers loose rock)

The main risk is a late-season storm system rolling through. Have a flexible backup day (May 18) if possible.
