# Mount Shasta — Permits & Logistics

> See also: [Itinerary](07-ITINERARY.md) for the day-by-day schedule, [Gear List](03-GEAR-LIST.md) for what to bring, and [Resources](08-RESOURCES.md) for rental shops and guide services.

## Permits Required

### 1. Summit Pass — $25/person
- **Required for:** All climbers traveling above 10,000 ft
- **Valid for:** Up to 3 consecutive days from purchase date
- **Purchase at:**
  - Mount Shasta Ranger District office (204 W Alma St, Mt Shasta, CA) — open M–F 8 AM–4:30 PM
  - Self-issue kiosk at Bunny Flat trailhead (when available, payment method varies)
- **Carry it:** Rangers check on the mountain
- **No lottery, no quota** — unlike Rainier or Whitney, you just buy it
- **Payment:** Check what's accepted — cash is safest at self-issue stations

### 2. Wilderness Permit — FREE
- **Required for:** All overnight camping in Mt. Shasta Wilderness
- **Self-issue:** Fill out form at trailhead permit box before entering
- **Not needed for:** Day-use-only climbers (but summit pass still required above 10,000 ft)

### 3. Human Waste Pack-Out Bags — REQUIRED
- **Mandatory above treeline.** WAG bags are free and available at the trailhead and ranger station.
- Pack out all human waste. There are no facilities above Horse Camp.

**Total cost per person: $25**
**Total for 3-person team: $75**

### Trailhead Access — Bunny Flat
- **Address:** Everitt Memorial Highway, Mount Shasta, CA
- **Elevation:** 6,950 ft
- **Road:** Everitt Memorial Highway (paved, 2WD accessible when open)
- **Status in mid-May:** Usually OPEN. The road typically opens by mid-April–early May. Check current status: (530) 926-4511 (Ranger District)
- **Parking:** Free. Lot fits ~50 cars. Can fill on weekends in peak season. Arrive Thursday/Friday for a Saturday summit.

## Camping

### Option 1: Helen Lake (10,443 ft) — RECOMMENDED
- Snow camping on the mountain — see [Snow Camping Deep Dive](09-DEEP-DIVES.md#2-snow-camping-at-helen-lake--the-full-breakdown) for full setup guide
- 3–4 hour approach from Bunny Flat
- Flat snow benches for tents
- Wind protection on south-facing slopes
- No water (melt snow)
- No facilities — [WAG bags required](09-DEEP-DIVES.md#3-human-waste-management-the-practical-guide)
- This is where 90% of Avalanche Gulch climbers camp

### Option 2: Horse Camp (7,880 ft)
- Sierra Club cabin (first-come, first-served donation-based)
- Spring water available
- Composting toilet
- More protected from wind
- Adds 2,500 ft and 2–3 hours to summit day (less ideal)

### Option 3: Town Basecamp
- Camp at a campground or hotel in Mt Shasta town
- Climb as a 1-day attempt from Bunny Flat (very long day — 12–16 hours)
- Not recommended unless very fit and acclimatized

### Pre-Trip Night (May 16)
Recommended: stay in the town of Mount Shasta the night before to acclimatize to moderate altitude.

## Timeline for Your Trip

| When | Action |
|------|--------|
| **Now (April)** | Book flights/rental car. Reserve [gear rentals](08-RESOURCES.md#gear-rental). |
| **1 week before (May 10)** | Check [weather forecast](02-WEATHER.md#weather-monitoring--check-these-before-and-during-trip), Shasta Avalanche Center, road status. |
| **3 days before (May 14)** | Confirm weather window. Pack gear. Ship anything needed. |
| **May 16 (Friday)** | Travel to Mt Shasta town. Check in. Visit ranger station for permits + WAG bags. Visit Fifth Season for any last gear/beta. Dinner. Sleep early. |
| **May 17 (Saturday)** | Drive to Bunny Flat. Hike to Helen Lake. Set camp. Rest. Eat. Sleep by 6–7 PM. |
| **May 18 (Sunday)** | Wake at midnight. Leave camp 1–2 AM. Summit 7–9 AM. Descend to Helen Lake by 10–11 AM. Break camp. Hike out to Bunny Flat. Drive home or celebrate in town. |

*Alternative: if weather is bad May 17, camp May 17 and summit May 18 with May 19 as backup.*
